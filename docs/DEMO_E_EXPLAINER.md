# Demo E — Detailed Explainer (Pilot)

*Companion to the figure in `figures/v2/demo_e_pilot.{pdf,png}`. Source code under `schmidt_demos/demo_e_cage4/`. This document explains, in plain language, what Demo E does and why. For the cross-cutting concepts (selection, reinjection, M, ρ, κ), see [`DEMO_A_EXPLAINER.md`](DEMO_A_EXPLAINER.md) §2. For the typed-gene defender architecture, MiniCAGE/CAGE-2 substrate, audit log, and NIST-grounded compliance predicates, see [`DEMO_B_EXPLAINER.md`](DEMO_B_EXPLAINER.md). Demo E is the generalization of the Demo B/C compliance story to CAGE Challenge 4 (CC4).*

*Design checkpoint:* [`DEMO_E_PLAN.md`](DEMO_E_PLAN.md) (locked design, with the section 6 audit-log-fitness pivot recorded after empirical discovery that CC4's `BlueFlatWrapper` broadcasts a single global reward to all blue agents).

## 1. Purpose

Demo E is the **bridge from Demo B/C's CAGE-2 results to a multi-defender, phased, multi-subnet cyber substrate (CAGE Challenge 4)**. Where Demos B and C demonstrated that the substrate's selection + reinjection machinery composes with a real cyber-defense benchmark, Demo E asks:

> Does the same substrate continue to produce monotone compliance trajectories under ρ on the harder CC4 benchmark — and does the m₃ separation-of-duty predicate, which was structurally N/A on single-defender CAGE-2, become measurable in the multi-defender CC4 setting?

The pilot answer (subject to the limitations enumerated below): **yes for monotone compliance under ρ; m₃ is structurally testable but the pilot's coarse signal-detection heuristic does not fire reliably enough to produce a clean number.** Under ρ=1, canonical-allele frequency at all three must-have loci is pinned at 1.000 and m₁ audit-integrity compliance is 1.000 across all five zones. Under ρ=0, canonical alleles drift to 0.24–0.39, m₁ falls to 0.73, m₂ containment falls to 0.13. The silent-erosion story from Demo B's single-defender substrate now plays on the five-defender CC4 substrate, with the same qualitative signature: canonical alleles get out-competed by cheaper non-canonical alleles under task-only selection, and reinjection reverses the erosion.

Demo E supports the substrate paper's generalization claim — *the substrate works on more than one cyber benchmark, without architectural changes* — and lays the groundwork for the production sweep that will become the first results paper specifically on CAGE Challenge 4.

## 2. Vocabulary recap

Cross-cutting concepts (defined in [`DEMO_A_EXPLAINER.md`](DEMO_A_EXPLAINER.md) §2): selection, reinjection, M, ρ, κ, canonical/variant alleles.

Substrate scaffolding (defined in [`DEMO_B_EXPLAINER.md`](DEMO_B_EXPLAINER.md)): the typed-gene defender architecture, the audit log, compliance predicates, fitness-proportional within-population selection, BEAR's locus-tagged TAGGED haploid crossover.

Three CC4-specific concepts introduced here:

- **5 zone defenders.** CC4's `EnterpriseScenarioGenerator` hardcodes five blue agents: `blue_agent_0..3` each defend one subnet (observation dim 92, action space `Discrete(82)` with 8 verbs × ~10 targets per subnet); `blue_agent_4` is HQ defending three subnets (observation dim 210, action space `Discrete(242)`). Not parameterizable without forking the scenario generator. Each plays simultaneously in every CC4 episode.
- **Phased mission.** Each CC4 observation begins with a mission-phase indicator at `obs[0]` taking values in `{0, 1, 2}`. The remaining flags are binary per-host / per-service. Demo E's defender reads the phase and applies phase-modulated behavior (see §6).
- **Action masking.** CC4's info dict carries an `action_mask` (list of bools) per blue agent. Defender-side, we always filter to legal actions per the mask; the defender selects a verb based on alleles, then picks the lowest-index legal action of that verb.

Two architecture choices specific to Demo E:

- **Five within-zone populations (Option A from DEMO_E_PLAN.md §1).** Each of the five zones has its own BEAR population of N_pop candidate defenders. Per generation, each zone's candidates are scored by running episodes where the candidate plays its zone and the other four zones are filled by random picks from those zones' colonies. Within-zone selection: fitness-proportional within each zone separately; no cross-zone gene flow. Net evolutionary state: 5 × N_pop defenders under selection. Alternatives considered (single shared population, team-genome) are documented in the plan as deferred.
- **Audit-log-derived fitness (Path B, plan §6).** The DEMO_E_PLAN initially assumed CC4 reward could be used per-zone. Empirical probing of `BlueFlatWrapper.step()` showed the same scalar reward is broadcast to every blue agent each tick (verified across three seeds; documented in the project memory `project_cc4_reward_is_global.md`). The forced alternative: derive both task and safety fitness from each defender's own audit log, not from CC4's reward dict. Task fitness = `-mean_over_ticks(zone_compromise_count)` where `zone_compromise_count` is the count of obs flags set in this defender's per-tick observation (everything after the phase indicator). Safety fitness = fraction of compliance predicates passed. Both are zone-local; selection within zone uses the fitness-proportional softmax weighted lottery.

## 3. What Demo E is *not* (pilot scope)

Demo E is a **pilot**, deliberately scoped down for fast iteration. Six explicit limits readers should hold in mind:

- **Pilot-scale: N_pop=15 per zone, 10 generations, 3 seeds, 50-tick episodes.** Production scope is N_pop=30+, longer episodes, full four-condition factorial, more seeds. The pilot is enough to demonstrate the qualitative signal; it is not enough for tight CIs.
- **Two pre-registered conditions, not four.** The pilot runs only `task_only__rho_0` and `task_only__rho_1`, the headline "silent erosion vs full enforcement" pair. The full design (also `task_only__rho_0_5` and `task_plus_safety__rho_0_5` with γ-weighted compliance) is deferred to the production sweep.
- **Rule-driven defender, not LLM-driven.** Same as Demos B and C: gene → action mapping is implemented as deterministic Python rules in `defender.py`. Year-1 work swaps this for the full BEAR retrieval + LLM-per-tick pipeline; the substrate's interface doesn't change.
- **Coarse m₃ signal-detection.** Cross-zone-traffic detection uses a `sum(obs[1:]) > 6` heuristic that fires rarely on 50-tick episodes. As a result, m₃ ≈ 0 in both conditions of the pilot. Production sweep needs a per-flag obs-schema decoder.
- **No LLM calls anywhere in Demo E.** Unlike Demo D's optional blend ablation, Demo E has no Anthropic-or-other API dependency in any code path.
- **One episode per candidate per generation.** The plan called for ~3 episodes per candidate to score fitness more robustly. The pilot uses 1 (each zone's candidates each play one episode, paired with random picks from other zones) to keep the wall-clock manageable. Production sweep should average over 3.

## 4. The substrate — CAGE Challenge 4

CC4 is the multi-defender successor to CAGE-2, distributed as part of the CybORG suite. The substrate Demo E exercises:

- **Network topology:** randomized per episode, but the agent-to-subnet mapping is fixed: `blue_agent_0..3` defend RESTRICTED_A, OPERATIONAL_A, RESTRICTED_B, OPERATIONAL_B respectively; `blue_agent_4` is HQ for the three HQ subnets (PUBLIC_ACCESS, ADMIN_NETWORK, OFFICE_NETWORK).
- **Episode loop:** each tick, the green agents (`EnterpriseGreenAgent`) simulate benign user traffic, the red agent (`FiniteStateRedAgent`) executes a phased attack, and all five blue agents act simultaneously. Phase changes at well-defined mission milestones. Pilot uses 50-tick episodes; CC4 default is 500.
- **Observation space:** `MultiDiscrete([3, 2, 2, ..., 2])`. Index 0 is the mission-phase indicator (cardinality 3); remaining flags are binary per-host / per-service. Length 92 for zone defenders, 210 for HQ.
- **Action space:** `Discrete(82)` for zone defenders, `Discrete(242)` for HQ. The 8 verbs are `Sleep`, `Monitor`, `Analyse`, `Remove`, `Restore`, `DeployDecoy`, `AllowTrafficZone`, `BlockTrafficZone`. Each action label parses as `<Verb> <subnet>_<host>` or `<Verb> <subnet> <- <subnet>` (for traffic-zone verbs). Some labels are prefixed `[Invalid]` for topology entries that don't exist this episode; the mask handles legality.
- **Reward:** per-tick scalar broadcast to every blue agent (verified empirically; see §2). Not used by Demo E's fitness — see §9.

CC4 installation lives at `third_party/cage-challenge-4/` (gitignored). A WSL venv at `.venv-cc4/` (Python 3.12.3) has `bear`, `pydantic`, `pyyaml`, and CC4 installed editable. Activation: `source .venv-cc4/bin/activate`. Demo E invokes from WSL because Windows-side Python has CybORG import quirks.

## 5. The genome — 8 loci (5 capability + 3 must-have)

| Locus | Category | Alleles | Canonical | In M? | Compliance predicate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `threat_threshold` | capability | `reactive` / `balanced` / `defensive` | `balanced` | — | — |
| `containment_style` | capability | `analyse_first` / `remove_first` / `restore_first` | `analyse_first` | — | — |
| `decoy_policy` | capability | `aggressive` / `conservative` / `none` | `conservative` | — | — |
| `traffic_default` | capability | `permissive` / `balanced` / `restrictive` | `balanced` | — | — |
| `phase_modulation` | capability | `stable` / `ramp_up` / `ramp_down` | `ramp_up` | — | — |
| `monitor_discipline` | safety | `monitor_all_ticks` / `monitor_on_signal` / `monitor_skip` | `monitor_all_ticks` | **Yes** | m₁_audit_integrity |
| `containment_protocol` | safety | `analyse_then_remove` / `remove_only` / `sleep_through` | `analyse_then_remove` | **Yes** (primary) | m₂_containment |
| `zone_boundary` | safety | `enforce_strict` / `enforce_on_threat` / `permissive_boundary` | `enforce_strict` | **Yes** | m₃_separation_of_duty |

Three structural differences from Demo B's 6-locus genome:

- **Eight loci instead of six.** Adds `traffic_default` (AllowTrafficZone/BlockTrafficZone preference, only meaningful with CC4's traffic verbs) and `phase_modulation` (CC4-specific phase behavior). Drops Demo B's `escalation_policy` and `suppression_policy` (their CC4 analogues fold into the more general `monitor_discipline`).
- **Three must-have loci, two of which are CC4-specific.** `monitor_discipline` is the closest analogue of Demo B's `audit_discipline` (m₁). `containment_protocol` is new — it's the primary predicate for Demo E, encoding the Analyse-then-Remove protocol that the silent-erosion story hinges on. `zone_boundary` is the m₃ separation-of-duty predicate, structurally testable for the first time on CC4's multi-defender substrate.
- **The `sleep_through` allele at `containment_protocol`.** Deliberately included as the silent-erosion winner: a defender that does nothing in response to compromise. Under task-only selection at ρ=0, this allele saves the action budget for non-containment moves and is therefore favored when reinjection is off — and that's exactly what the pilot data show.

## 6. Allele → action mapping — `BEARDefender._select_verb`

The defender's `_decide` step takes the current observation, the action mask, the tick, and the phase, and returns one of the 8 CC4 verbs. The full mapping lives in `defender.py:_select_verb` (lines ~225–290); here is the priority-ordered decision tree:

1. **Threat detection** uses `threat_threshold` and `phase_modulation`. `zone_compromise_count = sum(obs[1:])` is the threat signal; `threat_active` is true when count exceeds the allele-specific threshold (modulated by phase per `ramp_up`/`ramp_down`).

2. **Under threat: containment protocol (m₂)**. The `containment_protocol` allele decides:
   - `sleep_through` → return `Sleep` (the silent-erosion allele)
   - `remove_only` → return `Remove`
   - `analyse_then_remove` (canonical) → if a host was Analysed in the last k=3 ticks but not yet Removed, return `Remove`; otherwise return `Analyse` (or `Restore`/`Remove` under non-canonical `containment_style`)

3. **No threat, cross-zone signal: zone boundary (m₃)**. The `zone_boundary` allele decides:
   - `enforce_strict` (canonical) → return `BlockTrafficZone`
   - `enforce_on_threat` → defer (only blocks under simultaneous threat, handled in step 2)
   - `permissive_boundary` → fall through

4. **No threat, no cross-zone signal: monitor discipline (m₁)**. The `monitor_discipline` allele decides whether to emit `Monitor` (which produces the audit trail):
   - `monitor_all_ticks` (canonical) → return `Monitor`
   - `monitor_on_signal` → return `Monitor` only if any compromise flag is visible
   - `monitor_skip` → fall through (never emit Monitor)

5. **No threat, no signal, no Monitor: decoy policy**. `aggressive` → return `DeployDecoy`; `conservative` → return `DeployDecoy` early in the episode if no decoys yet placed; `none` → fall through.

6. **Otherwise: traffic default**. `permissive` → `AllowTrafficZone`; `restrictive` → `BlockTrafficZone`; `balanced` (canonical) → defer to mask.

7. **Otherwise: Sleep.**

If the preferred verb has no legal action under the mask, the defender falls through a fallback chain (`Monitor` → `Sleep` → other verbs), guaranteeing a legal action every tick. Action labels are pre-classified at defender construction via `_classify_action_label` (handles `[Invalid]` prefix stripping and target host/subnet extraction).

The key point: **capability alleles affect both task fitness and compliance** (they decide which actions get taken and against whom), while **must-have alleles affect compliance primarily** (they shape the audit log fields that compliance predicates consume). The decision-tree priority ensures m₁/m₂/m₃ behaviors are exercised under the conditions where their predicates apply.

## 7. The audit log

Every tick, each defender appends an `AuditEntry` to its episode-scoped audit log. The schema is deliberately richer than Demo B's to support both compliance predicates AND the per-zone task-fitness signal (the Path B pivot, plan §6):

```python
@dataclass
class AuditEntry:
    tick: int
    phase: int                          # 0/1/2 from obs[0]
    action_id: int
    action_verb: str                    # one of CC4_VERBS
    action_label: str                   # full label for traceability
    target_host: str | None
    target_subnet: str | None
    is_privileged: bool                 # Remove/Restore/DeployDecoy/BlockTrafficZone
    logged: bool                        # m1 monitor_discipline gate
    compromise_observed: bool           # any compromise flag set in obs
    analysed_target: bool               # this entry performed Analyse on a host
    removed_target: bool                # this entry performed Remove on an
                                        # Analysed host within k ticks
    cross_zone_traffic_signal: bool     # m3 signal heuristic
    traffic_blocked: bool               # m3 enforcement marker
    zone_compromise_count: int          # task-fitness signal (plan §6)
```

At episode end, the audit log is the substrate's record of "what this defender did, how it recorded itself doing it, AND what it observed." Compliance predicates in `compliance.py` consume the log to produce m₁/m₂/m₃ results; per-zone task fitness is computed downstream as `-mean(zone_compromise_count)` over the episode.

## 8. The compliance predicates — m₂ is primary

Three predicates, all testable on CC4 (m₃ is the first CC4-only predicate, structurally impossible on single-defender CAGE-2):

| Predicate | NIST / spec | Definition | Status in pilot |
|---|---|---|---|
| `m1_audit_integrity` | NIST SP 800-53 AU-2/AU-3 | ≥ 90% of privileged actions have `logged=True` | **Testable**; pilot result clean |
| `m2_containment` | NIST SP 800-53 IR-4 / OpenC2 CONTAIN | ≥ 80% of compromise-observed ticks are followed by Analyse-then-Remove within k=3 ticks | **Testable, primary**; pilot result clean |
| `m3_separation_of_duty` | NIST AC-5 | ≥ 80% of cross-zone-traffic signals are blocked within k=3 ticks | **Testable; pilot signal too sparse** — coarse heuristic rarely fires |

Per generation, `C_m(t) = fraction of zone's N_pop candidates whose audit log passes predicate m`. This is the y-axis for Panel A of the pilot figure (averaged across the 5 zones for the headline mean compliance).

The m₂ containment predicate is the **primary one for Demo E**. The DEMO_E_PLAN section 4 calls it out as the load-bearing predicate for the silent-erosion story: under ρ=0, defenders learn to skip the Analyse + Remove cycle (which costs ticks) in favor of cheap `sleep_through` behavior, and m₂ compliance falls. Under ρ=1, reinjection forces canonical `analyse_then_remove` and m₂ recovers — partially, because the predicate also requires the Analyse-then-Remove sequence to actually fire within the k-tick window, and even canonical defenders sometimes don't satisfy the sequencing constraint within the pilot's short 50-tick episodes.

## 9. Fitness — audit-log derived (Path B)

Per the DEMO_E_PLAN section 6 pivot:

```python
# task_only condition (pilot)
fitness = task_fitness_from_log(audit_log)
        = -mean_over_ticks(zone_compromise_count)

# task_plus_safety condition (not in pilot; for production)
fitness = task_fitness_from_log(audit_log) + γ * safety_fitness(audit_log)
        = -mean(zone_compromise_count) + γ * (#predicates_passed / 3)
```

Parent sampling within zone is fitness-proportional via softmax-style weights, identical to Demos B and C:

```python
weights = exp(selection_intensity · fitness)
probs   = weights / weights.sum()
parents = rng.choice(N_pop, size=N_pop, p=probs, replace=True)
```

After parent sampling, each pair breeds → mutates → reinjects via the shared `Colony` helpers (`_breed`, `_mutate`, `_enforce_must_have`). The shared `MustHaveSpec.rho` parameter is what differs between conditions; the rest of the pipeline is identical to Demo B's.

**Why audit-log fitness instead of CC4 reward:** CC4's `BlueFlatWrapper.step()` broadcasts the same scalar reward to every blue agent each tick. The reward calculator (`CybORG.Shared.BlueRewardMachine`) is per-team rather than per-agent — it computes a sum over all subnets' penalties and emits that scalar. Per-subnet reward decomposition exists internally but is summed away before any wrapper sees it. Three alternatives were considered (A1: subclass `BlueRewardMachine`; A3: monkey-patch with side channel; B: audit-log-derived fitness) — Path B was chosen because it keeps `third_party/` untouched, fits Demo E's compliance-narrative, and lets per-zone selection work on a clean per-zone observable that the defender directly produces. Full diagnosis is in `project_cc4_reward_is_global.md`.

## 10. Pre-registered conditions

| Condition | ρ | γ | In pilot? |
|---|---|---|---|
| `task_only__rho_0` | 0.0 | 0.0 | ✓ |
| `task_only__rho_0_5` | 0.5 | 0.0 | — (production) |
| `task_plus_safety__rho_0_5` | 0.5 | 0.5 | — (production) |
| `task_only__rho_1` | 1.0 | 0.0 | ✓ |

The pilot runs the two endpoints of the ρ sweep — the cleanest contrast for "does reinjection prevent the silent erosion?" The two intermediate conditions (`rho_0_5` and `task+safety` co-selection) are deferred to the production sweep. Pilot configuration: 3 replicate seeds × N_pop=15 per zone × 10 generations × 50-tick episodes against the `FiniteStateRedAgent`. Total: 6 replicate runs × 5 zones × 15 candidates × 10 generations = 4500 CC4 episodes; ~12 min wall-clock per replicate; ~75 min total.

## 11. The three panels — what each demonstrates

**Panel A — Mean compliance across 5 zones**

The y-axis is `C_m(t)`, averaged across the 5 zones × 3 seeds (one value per condition × predicate × generation). Solid lines: ρ=1 (full reinjection). Dashed lines: ρ=0. Predicates: m₁ (blue), m₂ (red), m₃ (purple).

- **m₁ (blue) under ρ=1:** pinned at 1.000 from generation 1 onward. Reinjection overwrites the `monitor_skip` and `monitor_on_signal` alleles to `monitor_all_ticks` in every offspring, and the defender's `_should_log` then emits `logged=True` on every privileged action.
- **m₁ (blue) under ρ=0:** drops from ~0.82 at generation 0 to ~0.73 by generation 9. The `monitor_all_ticks` canonical allele is out-competed by `monitor_skip` (which saves nothing per se but isn't fitness-rewarded either; drift dominates here since m₁ doesn't directly affect zone_compromise_count).
- **m₂ (red) under ρ=1:** rises from ~0.28 at generation 0 to ~0.40 by generation 9. Limited at 0.40 not by allele state (canonical is pinned by reinjection) but by the predicate's sequencing requirement — Analyse-then-Remove within k=3 ticks of each observed compromise — which even canonical defenders only satisfy partially within 50-tick pilot episodes.
- **m₂ (red) under ρ=0:** drops from ~0.28 to ~0.13. The canonical `analyse_then_remove` allele is out-competed by `sleep_through` (which saves the action budget for nothing-doing). This is the silent-erosion story on CC4.
- **m₃ (purple):** flat at 0.000 in both conditions. See §3 limit on the cross-zone-traffic heuristic.

**Panel B — Per-zone m₂ containment trajectories**

The y-axis is `C_{m₂}(t)` for each of the 5 zones separately, both conditions overlaid. Solid lines: ρ=1. Dashed: ρ=0. The five zones use different colors (blue_agent_0..3 are the four zone defenders, blue_agent_4 is HQ).

Reading: the trajectories under ρ=1 (solid) sit visibly above the ρ=0 trajectories (dashed) for every zone, with some zone-to-zone variation. HQ (blue_agent_4) generally has the lowest m₂ compliance under both conditions, consistent with its larger action space (242 vs 82) and three-subnet defensive scope making the Analyse-then-Remove sequence harder to satisfy. The zone-to-zone variation is meaningful: it shows that the within-zone selection architecture (Option A) genuinely produces different per-zone allele frequencies, which is the whole point of having five separate populations rather than one shared population.

**Panel C — Canonical-allele frequency at must-have loci**

The y-axis is the fraction of each zone's population carrying the canonical allele at each must-have locus, averaged across the 5 zones × 3 seeds. Solid lines: ρ=1. Dashed: ρ=0.

- **All three solid lines** pin at 1.000 from generation 1 onward. Reinjection overwrites variants every breeding step, so every offspring is canonical at every must-have locus. The `monitor_discipline` (purple), `containment_protocol` (red), and `zone_boundary` (blue) curves all overlap at the top.
- **The three dashed lines** start at ~0.50 (balanced across alleles at gen 0) and diverge:
  - `containment_protocol` (red dashed) drops to 0.39 by generation 9 — the silent-erosion locus; `sleep_through` wins.
  - `monitor_discipline` (purple dashed) drops to 0.24 — the steepest erosion; `monitor_skip` wins because non-monitoring is task-cheap.
  - `zone_boundary` (blue dashed) drops to 0.36 — similar pattern, `permissive_boundary` wins.

This panel is the cleanest visual of the substrate's enforcement story: **ρ=1 pins the canonical alleles; ρ=0 lets them drift down under task pressure.** The asymmetry between m₁/m₃ (which fall further under ρ=0) and m₂ (which falls less) reflects how directly each locus interacts with the task-fitness signal: monitoring and traffic-blocking aren't rewarded by `-zone_compromise_count`, so drift is unopposed; containment is at least loosely correlated with lower compromise counts (canonical Analyse-then-Remove reduces compromise), so selection partly resists the drift.

## 12. What Demo E shows that Demos B/C could not

Three things become measurable on CC4 that CAGE-2 structurally couldn't support:

1. **m₃ separation-of-duty is structurally testable.** CAGE-2 has one defender; the predicate has no scope. CC4 has five simultaneous defenders, each with subnet ownership, so the predicate has meaningful semantics. The pilot's coarse heuristic doesn't fire reliably enough to produce a clean number, but the testability is the architectural milestone; the production sweep with a refined obs-schema decoder is the next step for the actual m₃ measurement.
2. **Multi-defender population dynamics within an episode.** Demos B/C have population dynamics *across* the colony (N candidates per generation) but each *episode* is single-defender solo play. Demo E has within-episode multi-defender dynamics — five zone defenders coordinating against the same red team simultaneously. This is a structurally different test of the substrate's composition.
3. **Phased mission behavior.** CC4's `obs[0]` mission-phase indicator gives defenders information that CAGE-2 doesn't provide. The `phase_modulation` gene is a CC4-only locus that wouldn't have been testable on CAGE-2. The pilot doesn't isolate the phase-modulation effect (no ablation between `stable` and `ramp_up` canonical), but the production sweep would.

Demo E also reproduces the substrate's core compliance claim on a harder benchmark:

- The canonical-allele frequencies behave the same way as on CAGE-2: drift down under ρ=0, pin under ρ=1.
- The m₁ audit-integrity story is the same: monotone in ρ, headline pinning at 1.000 under ρ=1.
- The m₂ containment story is the same in direction (ρ=1 > ρ=0) and adds a CC4-specific predicate that the CAGE-2 demos didn't have.

That is the generalization claim the substrate paper's §5.5 makes empirical.

## 13. Open issues — what the pilot makes visible but doesn't resolve

- **m₃ signal detection.** The `_detect_cross_zone_signal` heuristic (`sum(obs[1:]) > 6`) is a placeholder. A refined implementation needs per-flag obs-schema knowledge to identify the specific bits that represent foreign-zone traffic. This is the highest-leverage refinement for the production sweep.
- **Episode length vs predicate satisfiability.** m₂ tops out at 0.40 under ρ=1 in part because the Analyse-then-Remove sequence requires multiple consecutive ticks and the pilot only has 50 ticks. Production sweep at 100–200 ticks should produce m₂ closer to 1.0 under ρ=1.
- **Single episode per candidate.** The pilot uses 1 episode per candidate per generation; the plan called for 3. Variance bands would tighten with more episodes per candidate.
- **N_pop and seeds.** Pilot uses N_pop=15 per zone, 3 seeds. Production scope is N_pop=30+, 10+ seeds — necessary for tight CIs.
- **The other two pre-registered conditions.** `task_only__rho_0_5` (intermediate enforcement) and `task_plus_safety__rho_0_5` (γ-weighted compliance co-selection) are not in the pilot. Production sweep should run the full four-condition factorial.
- **HQ asymmetry.** `blue_agent_4` has a 242-action space vs the zone defenders' 82-action space, and three subnets vs one. The pilot collapses HQ into the same defender architecture; production should probably distinguish HQ-specific behavior.

## 14. The takeaway

Demo E's pilot headline, in one sentence: **the substrate that produced monotone compliance under ρ on CAGE-2 (Demos B/C) produces the same monotone compliance under ρ on CAGE-4, without architectural changes to the gene schema, the breeding primitive, or the must-have reinjection mechanism.**

The five-line version:

- 5 within-zone populations (one per CC4 blue agent), N_pop=15, 10 generations, 3 seeds.
- Under ρ=1, all three must-have loci are pinned canonical (1.000) and m₁ audit-integrity is 1.000 across all 5 zones.
- Under ρ=0, the canonical alleles drift down to 0.24–0.39, m₁ falls to 0.73, m₂ containment falls to 0.13 — the silent-erosion story on the harder substrate.
- m₃ separation-of-duty is structurally testable on CC4 for the first time, but the pilot's coarse signal-detection heuristic does not fire reliably enough to produce a clean number; refinement deferred to the production sweep.
- The substrate composes correctly with two cyber substrates that differ in defender count, observation dimension, action space size, and mission structure.

For the substrate paper, Demo E's pilot supports the generalization claim in §5.5 without claiming production-scale results. The natural follow-up is a CC4-centric paper running the full four-condition factorial at production scope, addressing the m₃ obs-schema refinement, and adding LLM-driven defender ablations.
